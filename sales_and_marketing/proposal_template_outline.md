# Proposal Template Outline

## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive template and guidance for preparing client proposals for aquaculture projects. A well-structured proposal clearly communicates our understanding, solution, value proposition, and commercial terms.

---

## Proposal Document Structure

### Standard Proposal Sections

| Section | Pages | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Cover Letter | 1 | Personal introduction and summary |
| Executive Summary | 1-2 | Key points for decision makers |
| Understanding | 2-3 | Demonstrate we understand client needs |
| Technical Proposal | 5-10 | Solution description and approach |
| Team and Experience | 2-3 | Qualifications and references |
| Project Approach | 2-4 | Methodology and timeline |
| Financial Proposal | 2-4 | Pricing and payment terms |
| Terms and Conditions | 1-2 | Legal and commercial terms |
| Appendices | Variable | Supporting materials |

**Total Length:** 15-30 pages (excluding appendices)

## Proposal Best Practices

### Before Writing

- [ ] Complete discovery and understand requirements
- [ ] Identify decision makers and influencers
- [ ] Understand budget and timeline constraints
- [ ] Clarify evaluation criteria
- [ ] Identify competition (if known)

### Writing Guidelines

- **Be client-focused:** Write about their needs, not our capabilities
- **Be specific:** Avoid vague language; use concrete numbers and examples
- **Be visual:** Use tables, charts, and diagrams to aid comprehension
- **Be concise:** Respect reader's time; executive summary should stand alone
- **Be professional:** Consistent formatting, error-free, branded
- **Be honest:** Clearly state assumptions, exclusions, and limitations

### After Submission

- [ ] Confirm receipt
- [ ] Offer to present/discuss
- [ ] Follow up per agreed timeline
- [ ] Address questions promptly
- [ ] Track in CRM
