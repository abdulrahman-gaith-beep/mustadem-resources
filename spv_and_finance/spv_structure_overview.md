# SPV Structure Overview

## Overview

This document provides guidance on Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) structures for aquaculture investments in Saudi Arabia. It covers legal entity options, formation requirements, governance structures, tax considerations, and investor protections.

---

## Legal Structure Options

### 1. Limited Liability Company (LLC) - الشركة ذات المسؤولية المحدودة

**Overview:**
The most common structure for mid-sized aquaculture projects, offering flexibility and limited liability protection.

**Key Characteristics:**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Arabic Name | Sharikat That Masouliya Mahdouda |
| Minimum Capital | SAR 100,000 (typical for aquaculture: SAR 500K-5M) |
| Minimum Partners | 1 (single member LLC allowed) |
| Maximum Partners | 50 |
| Foreign Ownership | Up to 100% (with MISA license) |
| Liability | Limited to capital contribution |
| Management | By managers (partners or external) |
| Transfer of Shares | Subject to partner approval rights |

**Advantages:**
- Simpler formation and administration than joint stock
- Flexible profit distribution arrangements
- Limited liability protection
- 100% foreign ownership permitted
- Suitable for most aquaculture investments

**Disadvantages:**
- Cannot publicly trade shares
- May be less recognized internationally
- Partner transfer restrictions can complicate exits

**Best For:**
- Single investor projects
- Small to medium partnerships (2-10 partners)
- Family investments
- Projects under SAR 100M investment

---

### 2. Joint Stock Company (JSC) - الشركة المساهمة

**Overview:**
Suitable for larger projects with multiple investors or future public offering plans.

**Key Characteristics:**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Arabic Name | Sharikat Musahima |
| Types | Closed JSC or Public JSC |
| Minimum Capital | SAR 500,000 (Closed), SAR 10M (Public) |
| Minimum Shareholders | 2 (Closed), 5 (Public) |
| Foreign Ownership | Up to 100% (with MISA license) |
| Liability | Limited to share value |
| Management | Board of Directors (minimum 3 members) |
| Transfer of Shares | Generally freely transferable |

**Advantages:**
- Familiar structure for international investors
- Easier share transfers
- Can convert to public company
- Clear governance framework
- Suitable for large capital raises

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher formation costs
- More complex governance requirements
- Annual audit required
- More regulatory oversight

**Best For:**
- Large projects (SAR 100M+)
- Multiple institutional investors
- Future IPO consideration
- International joint ventures

---

### 3. Limited Partnership - الشركة التضامنية المحدودة

**Overview:**
Less common, but can be useful for specific investor structures.

**Key Characteristics:**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| General Partners | Unlimited liability, manage business |
| Limited Partners | Liability limited to contribution, passive |
| Management | By general partner(s) |
| Profit Sharing | Per partnership agreement |

**Advantages:**
- Flexible allocation of profits and losses
- Clear distinction between operators and investors
- Pass-through taxation possible

**Disadvantages:**
- General partner has unlimited liability
- Less familiar to some investors
- May require additional structuring

**Best For:**
- Operator + passive investor arrangements
- Funds with external GP structure

---

### 4. Branch of Foreign Company - فرع شركة أجنبية

**Overview:**
Allows a foreign company to operate directly in Saudi Arabia without creating a separate entity.

**Key Characteristics:**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Legal Status | Extension of parent company |
| Liability | Parent company fully liable |
| Management | By branch manager |
| Capital | No minimum (but must have resources) |
| Taxation | Taxed on Saudi-source income |

**Advantages:**
- Direct control by parent
- No local partner required
- Simpler repatriation of profits

**Disadvantages:**
- Parent liability for branch activities
- May face operational restrictions
- Not a separate legal entity for local contracting

**Best For:**
- International operators expanding to Saudi Arabia
- Technical service provision
- Preliminary market entry

---

### Comparison Summary

| Criteria | LLC | Closed JSC | Branch |
|----------|-----|------------|--------|
| Formation Complexity | Low | Medium | Low |
| Formation Time | 2-4 weeks | 4-8 weeks | 4-6 weeks |
| Formation Cost | SAR 15-30K | SAR 50-100K | SAR 20-40K |
| Foreign Ownership | 100% | 100% | 100% |
| Governance | Flexible | Formal Board | Parent-controlled |
| Exit/Transfer | Restricted | Easier | N/A |
| Best For | Most projects | Large/institutional | Services/expansion |

## Saudi Arabia Company Formation

### MISA (Ministry of Investment) Process

For foreign investment, Ministry of Investment of Saudi Arabia (MISA) approval is required.

**MISA License Types:**

| License Type | Description | Minimum Investment |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Industrial | Manufacturing/processing | SAR 1M |
| Agricultural | Including aquaculture | SAR 1M |
| Services | Consulting, technical | SAR 500K |
| Trading | Wholesale/retail | SAR 26M (wholesale) |

**Application Requirements:**

1. **Company Documents:**
   - Articles of incorporation (home country)
   - Certificate of good standing
   - Board resolution authorizing Saudi investment
   - Financial statements (2-3 years)
   - Company profile

2. **Project Documents:**
   - Business plan
   - Investment plan
   - Feasibility study
   - Environmental assessment (if required)

3. **Personal Documents (for shareholders/directors):**
   - Passport copies
   - Professional CVs
   - Police clearance certificates

**Timeline:**
- Application preparation: 2-4 weeks
- MISA review and approval: 2-4 weeks
- Company registration: 1-2 weeks
- **Total: 5-10 weeks**

---

### Capital Requirements

**Minimum Capital by Structure:**

| Entity Type | Minimum Capital | Recommended for Aquaculture |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| LLC | SAR 100,000 | SAR 1-5 million |
| Closed JSC | SAR 500,000 | SAR 5-20 million |
| Public JSC | SAR 10 million | N/A for typical projects |

**Capital Contribution:**
- Can be cash or in-kind (equipment, IP, land)
- Cash capital must be deposited in Saudi bank
- Capital increase possible after formation

---

### Foreign Ownership Rules

**100% Foreign Ownership Permitted:**
- Aquaculture and agriculture projects
- With valid MISA investment license
- Subject to specific activity requirements

**Saudization Requirements:**
- Nitaqat system applies (employment quotas)
- Percentage depends on company size
- Aquaculture may receive preferential treatment

---

### Free Zone Options

**KAEC (King Abdullah Economic City):**
- Industrial and logistics zones
- Streamlined licensing
- Custom duty advantages

**Neom:**
- Future zone with advanced regulations
- Potential for aquaculture projects
- Still developing

**RCJY (Royal Commission for Jubail & Yanbu):**
- Industrial zones with infrastructure
- Water access for marine applications

## Governance Structure

### Board of Directors

**Composition:**

| Role | Responsibility | Recommended |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| Chairman | Lead board, represent company | Major shareholder |
| Managing Director/CEO | Day-to-day operations | Professional manager |
| Independent Directors | Objective oversight | Industry experts |
| Investor Representatives | Protect investor interests | Per shareholding |

**Typical Board Size:** 3-7 members

**Board Meetings:**
- Minimum: Quarterly
- Quorum: Majority unless otherwise specified
- Decisions: Majority vote (reserved matters may require supermajority)

---

### Shareholder Agreements

**Key Provisions:**

**1. Share Transfer:**
| Provision | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Right of First Refusal (ROFR) | Other shareholders can match third-party offers |
| Pre-emptive Rights | Right to maintain ownership percentage |
| Lock-up Period | Restrictions on sale for defined period |
| Permitted Transfers | Allowed transfers (affiliates, family) |

**2. Reserved Matters (Requiring Supermajority):**
- Amendment of constitutional documents
- Major capital expenditure (>SAR X)
- Debt above threshold
- Related party transactions
- Change of business
- Dividend policy changes
- Appointment/removal of key executives
- Sale or merger of company

**3. Deadlock Resolution:**
- Escalation to senior executives
- Mediation
- Buy-sell provisions (shotgun clause)
- Arbitration

---

### Voting Rights

**Standard Structure:**
- One share = one vote
- Decisions by majority
- Reserved matters by supermajority (75% or 80%)

**Alternative Structures:**
- Weighted voting rights
- Class shares (A, B with different rights)
- Management shares

**Protective Provisions for Minority Investors:**
- Board representation thresholds
- Consent rights for major decisions
- Information rights
- Inspection rights

---

### Dividend Policy

**Typical Provisions:**

| Aspect | Common Approach |
|--------|-----------------|
| Distribution Trigger | Distributable profits above threshold |
| Frequency | Annual (can be quarterly) |
| Amount | Per board discretion within policy |
| Priority | Debt service, reserves, then dividends |
| Preferred Returns | Some investors may have preferred return |

**Example Policy:**
> "The Company shall distribute annually not less than 50% of net profits after (i) deduction of required legal reserves, (ii) repayment of any outstanding shareholder loans, and (iii) maintenance of minimum working capital of SAR X million, unless otherwise agreed by shareholders holding 75% of shares."

---

### Exit Provisions

**Exit Mechanisms:**

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Trade Sale | Sale to strategic buyer |
| IPO | Public offering (for suitable projects) |
| Management Buyout | Sale to management team |
| Put Option | Right to require buyout at defined terms |
| Drag-Along | Majority can force minority to sell |
| Tag-Along | Minority can join majority sale |

**Typical Timeline:**
- Lock-up period: 3-5 years
- Target exit: 7-10 years
- Mandatory exit provisions: May trigger after 10 years

## Tax Considerations

### Corporate Income Tax

**Standard Rate:** 20% on net taxable income

**Applicable To:**
- Net profits from Saudi operations
- Foreign companies on Saudi-source income
- GCC nationals and companies in Saudi Arabia

**Tax Incentives:**
- Agricultural sector may qualify for reduced rates
- Free zone entities may have exemptions
- ADF-financed projects may have incentives

---

### Value Added Tax (VAT)

**Standard Rate:** 15%

**Application:**
- Most goods and services
- Equipment purchases
- Operating supplies

**Exemptions/Zero-Rating:**
- Some food products
- Exports
- Certain agricultural inputs

**Registration:**
- Required if annual turnover exceeds SAR 375,000
- Voluntary registration below threshold

---

### Withholding Tax

**Rates on Payments to Non-Residents:**

| Payment Type | Rate |
|--------------|------|
| Dividends | 5% |
| Interest | 5% |
| Royalties | 15% |
| Technical Services | 5% |
| Management Fees | 20% |

**Double Tax Treaties:**
Saudi Arabia has treaties with many countries that may reduce rates.

---

### Zakat

**Applicable To:**
- Saudi and GCC shareholders
- Rate: 2.5% of zakat base (adjusted net worth)

**Not Applicable To:**
- Foreign shareholder portions (subject to income tax instead)

---

### Transfer Pricing

**Requirements:**
- Arm's length pricing for related party transactions
- Documentation requirements for large companies
- Advance pricing agreements available

**Relevance:**
- Transactions with foreign parent/affiliates
- Management fees, royalties, intercompany loans
- Equipment purchases from related parties

---

## Investor Protections

### Minority Rights

**Statutory Protections:**
- Right to participate in general meetings
- Right to receive financial information
- Right to challenge oppressive conduct
- Right to proportional treatment

**Contractual Protections (via Shareholder Agreement):**
- Board representation (typically at 10-20% ownership)
- Information and inspection rights
- Consent rights for major decisions
- Anti-dilution protection

---

### Tag-Along Rights

**Definition:**
Right of minority shareholders to participate in a sale by majority shareholders on the same terms.

**Standard Terms:**
- Triggered when majority (>50%) sells
- Minority can sell same percentage
- Same price per share
- Same terms and conditions

**Example Clause:**
> "If shareholders holding more than 50% of shares propose to transfer their shares to a third party, all other shareholders shall have the right to participate in such sale on the same terms, selling the same proportion of their shares."

---

### Drag-Along Rights

**Definition:**
Right of majority shareholders to compel minority shareholders to sell in a transaction.

**Standard Terms:**
- Triggered at high threshold (75-90%)
- Same price per share as majority
- Minority must be released from guarantees
- Reasonable notice period

**Purpose:**
Ensures majority can complete transaction without minority blocking.

---

### Anti-Dilution Protection

**Types:**

| Type | Protection |
|------|------------|
| Pre-emptive Rights | Right to purchase shares in new issuance |
| Full Ratchet | Adjustment for any lower-priced issuance |
| Weighted Average | Adjustment based on relative impact |

**Common Approach:**
- Pre-emptive rights standard in shareholder agreements
- Full anti-dilution rare (investor-friendly)
- Weighted average common for venture investments

---

### Information Rights

**Standard Provisions:**
- Annual audited financial statements
- Quarterly management accounts
- Annual budget before fiscal year
- Monthly production reports
- Notice of material events
- Board meeting materials

**Audit Rights:**
- Right to conduct independent audit
- Access to books and records
- Usually at investor's cost if beyond normal reporting

## Formation Process Summary

### Step-by-Step Formation (LLC with Foreign Investment)

| Step | Activity | Duration | Notes |
|------|----------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Engage legal/formation agent | Week 0 | Select experienced advisor |
| 2 | Prepare MISA application | Week 1-2 | Gather documents |
| 3 | Submit MISA application | Week 3 | Online system |
| 4 | MISA review and approval | Week 4-6 | May require clarifications |
| 5 | Draft/finalize constitutional documents | Week 4-6 | Articles of Association |
| 6 | Open bank account | Week 6-7 | Deposit capital |
| 7 | Company registration (CR) | Week 7-8 | Commercial Registration |
| 8 | Additional registrations | Week 8-10 | GOSI, Zakat/Tax, Municipality |
| 9 | Operational licenses | Week 8-12 | MEWA aquaculture license |

**Total Timeline:** 8-12 weeks
