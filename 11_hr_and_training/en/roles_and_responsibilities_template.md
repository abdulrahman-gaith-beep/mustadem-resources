# Roles and Responsibilities Template

## Overview

This document defines the key roles, responsibilities, and organizational structure for RAS (Recirculating Aquaculture System) aquaculture operations. It includes job descriptions, qualification requirements, and RACI matrices for key processes.

## Job Descriptions

### 1. Farm Manager

**Job Title:** Farm Manager  
**Department:** Operations  
**Reports To:** Operations Director / General Manager  
**Direct Reports:** Aquaculture Supervisor, Maintenance Technician, Admin/QC Specialist

---

**Position Summary:**

The Farm Manager is responsible for the overall operation of the RAS facility, including production planning, staff management, financial performance, regulatory compliance, and stakeholder communications. This role requires deep technical knowledge of aquaculture combined with strong leadership and business management skills.

---

**Key Responsibilities:**

**Production Management (40%)**
- Develop and execute annual production plan
- Monitor and optimize production KPIs (FCR, growth rates, survival)
- Oversee fish health management and biosecurity programs
- Manage inventory (fish stock, feed, supplies)
- Coordinate stocking and harvest schedules with market demand
- Ensure SOPs are followed and updated as needed

**Staff Management (25%)**
- Recruit, train, and develop farm personnel
- Create and manage work schedules
- Conduct performance reviews and provide feedback
- Foster positive team culture and morale
- Ensure compliance with labor regulations
- Maintain adequate staffing levels

**Financial Management (15%)**
- Develop and manage operating budget
- Monitor costs and implement cost controls
- Approve purchases within authority limits
- Review financial reports and variance analyses
- Contribute to capital planning and investment decisions

**Regulatory and Compliance (10%)**
- Ensure compliance with MEWA, SFDA, and environmental regulations
- Maintain required permits and licenses
- Prepare for and manage regulatory inspections
- Oversee certification programs (BAP, etc.)

**Stakeholder Relations (10%)**
- Report to senior management and investors
- Coordinate with clients and buyers
- Manage vendor and supplier relationships
- Represent facility in industry events

---

**Qualifications:**

**Required:**
- Bachelor's degree in Aquaculture, Marine Biology, Fisheries, or related field
- Minimum 7 years experience in aquaculture operations
- Minimum 3 years in a supervisory or management role
- Demonstrated expertise in RAS technology
- Strong understanding of fish health and water quality management
- Proficiency in English (Arabic preferred for Saudi operations)
- Valid driver's license

**Preferred:**
- Master's degree in Aquaculture or related field
- Experience with species relevant to the facility
- Previous experience in Saudi Arabia or GCC region
- Knowledge of BAP or ASC certification requirements
- Financial management training or certification

---

**Skills and Competencies:**

| Competency | Level Required |
|------------|----------------|
| Technical Aquaculture Knowledge | Expert |
| Leadership and Team Management | Expert |
| Problem-Solving and Decision Making | Expert |
| Communication (Written and Verbal) | Advanced |
| Financial Acumen | Advanced |
| Project Management | Advanced |
| Regulatory Knowledge | Advanced |
| Computer Skills (Office, SCADA) | Intermediate |

---

**Working Conditions:**
- Split time between office and production floor (60/40)
- On-call availability for emergencies
- Occasional weekend and holiday work required
- Physical ability to conduct facility inspections

---

**Salary Range:** SAR 15,000-25,000/month (based on experience and facility size)

---

### 2. Aquaculture Supervisor

**Job Title:** Aquaculture Supervisor / Shift Supervisor  
**Department:** Production  
**Reports To:** Farm Manager  
**Direct Reports:** Aquaculture Technicians, General Workers

---

**Position Summary:**

The Aquaculture Supervisor oversees daily production operations, supervises technical staff, and ensures adherence to operational protocols. This role serves as the primary point of contact for shift operations and bridges farm management with front-line staff.

---

**Key Responsibilities:**

**Daily Operations (50%)**
- Supervise all daily production activities
- Ensure water quality parameters are maintained
- Oversee feeding operations and feed management
- Monitor fish health and behavior
- Coordinate with maintenance for equipment issues
- Manage shift handovers and communication

**Staff Supervision (25%)**
- Direct and assign tasks to technicians and workers
- Train new staff on SOPs and procedures
- Monitor staff performance and provide feedback
- Ensure safety compliance on shift
- Manage schedules and coverage

**Quality and Documentation (15%)**
- Verify accuracy of data recording
- Review and approve daily logs
- Prepare shift and weekly reports
- Identify and report anomalies
- Ensure SOP compliance

**Problem Resolution (10%)**
- First-line troubleshooting for operational issues
- Escalate complex issues to Farm Manager
- Implement corrective actions
- Participate in root cause analysis

---

**Qualifications:**

**Required:**
- Diploma or Bachelor's degree in Aquaculture, Animal Science, or related field
- Minimum 4 years experience in aquaculture operations
- Demonstrated competency in RAS systems
- Experience supervising small teams
- Fluency in English (Arabic preferred)

**Preferred:**
- Bachelor's degree in Aquaculture or related field
- Previous supervisor experience
- Experience with species at the facility
- First aid certification

---

**Skills and Competencies:**

| Competency | Level Required |
|------------|----------------|
| Technical Aquaculture Knowledge | Advanced |
| Team Leadership | Advanced |
| Problem-Solving | Advanced |
| Communication | Intermediate |
| Data Recording and Reporting | Intermediate |
| Computer Skills | Intermediate |

---

**Working Conditions:**
- Primarily on production floor (80%)
- Shift work (rotations may apply)
- Physical demands: standing, walking, lifting up to 25 kg
- Exposure to water, fish handling

---

**Salary Range:** SAR 10,000-15,000/month

### 3. Aquaculture Technician

**Job Title:** Aquaculture Technician  
**Department:** Production  
**Reports To:** Aquaculture Supervisor  
**Direct Reports:** None (may supervise workers)

---

**Position Summary:**

The Aquaculture Technician performs hands-on daily operations including water quality monitoring, feeding, fish health observation, and routine maintenance. This role is critical for maintaining optimal growing conditions and early detection of issues.

---

**Key Responsibilities:**

**Water Quality Management (30%)**
- Conduct water quality testing per SOP (temperature, DO, pH, ammonia, nitrite)
- Record all measurements accurately
- Identify and report deviations from normal ranges
- Assist with water treatment adjustments
- Calibrate testing equipment

**Feeding Operations (25%)**
- Calculate and prepare daily feeding rations
- Execute feeding schedule according to SOP
- Observe and record feeding response
- Manage feed storage and inventory
- Report feeding anomalies

**Fish Health Monitoring (20%)**
- Conduct regular tank inspections
- Observe fish behavior and appearance
- Collect and record mortality data
- Assist with sampling and health assessments
- Report health concerns promptly

**General Operations (15%)**
- Assist with stocking and harvesting
- Perform routine tank cleaning
- Support grading operations
- Maintain cleanliness of work areas
- Handle routine equipment checks

**Documentation (10%)**
- Complete daily logs and forms accurately
- Enter data into digital systems
- Maintain organized records
- Prepare daily shift summaries

---

**Qualifications:**

**Required:**
- High school diploma or equivalent
- Minimum 1 year experience in aquaculture or animal husbandry
- Basic understanding of water chemistry
- Physical ability to perform manual tasks
- Willingness to work shifts

**Preferred:**
- Diploma in Aquaculture or related field
- Experience with RAS systems
- Computer literacy
- First aid training

---

**Skills and Competencies:**

| Competency | Level Required |
|------------|----------------|
| Technical Aquaculture Knowledge | Intermediate |
| Attention to Detail | Advanced |
| Physical Capability | Advanced |
| Teamwork | Intermediate |
| Basic Math and Recording | Intermediate |
| Reliability | Advanced |

---

**Working Conditions:**
- Full-time on production floor
- Shift work (days, evenings, weekends, holidays)
- Physical demands: standing 8+ hours, lifting up to 20 kg
- Wet environment, exposure to fish and chemicals

---

**Salary Range:** SAR 6,000-10,000/month

---

### 4. Maintenance Technician

**Job Title:** Maintenance Technician / Maintenance Specialist  
**Department:** Maintenance  
**Reports To:** Farm Manager / Maintenance Manager  
**Direct Reports:** None

---

**Position Summary:**

The Maintenance Technician is responsible for the preventive and corrective maintenance of all facility equipment, ensuring reliable operation of life-support systems and minimizing unplanned downtime.

---

**Key Responsibilities:**

**Preventive Maintenance (40%)**
- Execute scheduled maintenance per maintenance plan
- Perform daily equipment inspections
- Lubricate, clean, and adjust equipment
- Replace consumables (filters, belts, UV lamps)
- Document all maintenance activities

**Corrective Maintenance (30%)**
- Diagnose and repair equipment failures
- Respond to emergency breakdowns promptly
- Fabricate or source replacement parts
- Coordinate with vendors for complex repairs
- Minimize downtime for critical systems

**Systems Monitoring (15%)**
- Monitor SCADA and alarm systems
- Verify proper operation of automation
- Calibrate sensors and instruments
- Test backup systems regularly
- Maintain equipment performance logs

**Facilities Support (15%)**
- Maintain building systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical)
- Support construction and installation projects
- Manage spare parts inventory
- Assist with equipment procurement
- Maintain workshop organization

---

**Qualifications:**

**Required:**
- Diploma in Electrical, Mechanical, or Industrial Maintenance
- Minimum 3 years experience in industrial maintenance
- Experience with pumps, motors, and control systems
- Basic electrical troubleshooting capability
- Valid driver's license

**Preferred:**
- Bachelor's degree in Engineering or related field
- Experience in aquaculture or water treatment facilities
- PLC/SCADA experience
- Welding and fabrication skills
- Refrigeration/HVAC certification

---

**Skills and Competencies:**

| Competency | Level Required |
|------------|----------------|
| Mechanical Systems | Advanced |
| Electrical Systems | Intermediate-Advanced |
| Troubleshooting | Advanced |
| Documentation | Intermediate |
| Safety Practices | Advanced |
| Computer Skills (SCADA, CMMS) | Intermediate |

---

**Working Conditions:**
- Split between workshop and facility floor
- On-call for emergencies (rotation)
- Physical demands: climbing, lifting, confined spaces
- Exposure to electrical hazards, chemicals

---

**Salary Range:** SAR 7,000-12,000/month

### 5. Quality Control Specialist

**Job Title:** Quality Control Specialist / QC Manager  
**Department:** Quality Assurance  
**Reports To:** Farm Manager  
**Direct Reports:** QC Assistants (if applicable)

---

**Position Summary:**

The Quality Control Specialist ensures product quality, food safety, and regulatory compliance throughout the production process. This role manages certification programs, conducts audits, and oversees laboratory testing.

---

**Key Responsibilities:**

**Quality Assurance (35%)**
- Develop and maintain quality management system
- Conduct internal audits and inspections
- Implement corrective and preventive actions
- Manage non-conformance reports
- Train staff on quality procedures

**Food Safety (25%)**
- Implement HACCP or equivalent food safety system
- Monitor critical control points
- Manage product sampling and testing
- Coordinate with SFDA for compliance
- Handle product recalls if necessary

**Certification Management (20%)**
- Maintain BAP, ASC, or other certifications
- Prepare for external audits
- Compile documentation for certification bodies
- Address audit findings
- Stay current with certification requirements

**Laboratory Operations (10%)**
- Oversee on-site water and product testing
- Maintain laboratory equipment and supplies
- Coordinate with external laboratories
- Ensure testing accuracy and reliability

**Documentation and Reporting (10%)**
- Maintain quality records and traceability
- Prepare quality reports for management
- Analyze quality trends and metrics
- Support regulatory submissions

---

**Qualifications:**

**Required:**
- Bachelor's degree in Food Science, Microbiology, Quality Assurance, or related field
- Minimum 3 years experience in quality control (food/aquaculture preferred)
- Knowledge of HACCP, food safety regulations
- Experience with certification programs
- Strong documentation and audit skills

**Preferred:**
- Master's degree in relevant field
- Lead auditor certification (ISO, BAP, ASC)
- Previous aquaculture industry experience
- SFDA regulatory experience

---

**Skills and Competencies:**

| Competency | Level Required |
|------------|----------------|
| Quality Management Systems | Expert |
| Food Safety and HACCP | Advanced |
| Auditing | Advanced |
| Laboratory Techniques | Intermediate |
| Regulatory Knowledge | Advanced |
| Communication and Training | Advanced |

---

**Salary Range:** SAR 9,000-15,000/month

### 6. General Worker

**Job Title:** General Worker / Farm Hand  
**Department:** Operations  
**Reports To:** Aquaculture Supervisor / Technician  
**Direct Reports:** None

---

**Position Summary:**

General Workers provide essential support for daily farm operations including cleaning, feeding assistance, harvesting, material handling, and other manual tasks as assigned.

---

**Key Responsibilities:**

**Cleaning and Sanitation (30%)**
- Clean tanks, equipment, and work areas
- Assist with disinfection procedures
- Maintain cleanliness of facility
- Dispose of waste properly

**Feeding Support (25%)**
- Assist with feed preparation and delivery
- Handle feed storage and rotation
- Clean feeding equipment
- Report any feed issues

**Harvest and Handling (20%)**
- Assist with fish harvesting
- Support grading and sorting operations
- Handle fish with care
- Prepare fish for transport

**Material Handling (15%)**
- Receive and store supplies
- Move materials as needed
- Maintain organized storage areas
- Assist with inventory counts

**General Support (10%)**
- Perform other tasks as assigned
- Support maintenance activities
- Assist technicians as needed
- Report safety hazards

---

**Qualifications:**

**Required:**
- Ability to perform physical labor
- Reliability and punctuality
- Willingness to learn
- Basic communication skills

**Preferred:**
- Previous experience in farming, fishing, or manual labor
- Ability to read and write
- Driver's license

---

**Working Conditions:**
- Full-time on production floor
- Shift work required
- Heavy physical demands
- Wet environment, fish handling

---

**Salary Range:** SAR 3,500-6,000/month

## RACI Matrix for Key Processes

### How to Read RACI Matrix

| Letter | Role | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| R | Responsible | Does the work to complete the task |
| A | Accountable | Ultimately answerable for correct completion |
| C | Consulted | Provides input and expertise |
| I | Informed | Kept updated on progress and outcomes |

---
