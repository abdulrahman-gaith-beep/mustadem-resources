# Project Lifecycle Overview

## Overview

This document outlines the complete lifecycle for RAS (Recirculating Aquaculture System) project development, from initial concept through full production. It provides a structured framework for planning, executing, and delivering aquaculture projects successfully.

---

## Project Phases Summary

| Phase | Duration | Primary Deliverables | Key Decision |
|-------|----------|---------------------|--------------|
| 1. Pre-Feasibility | 2-4 weeks | Concept assessment, initial analysis | Proceed to feasibility? |
| 2. Feasibility Study | 1-2 months | Technical & financial assessment | Investment decision |
| 3. Detailed Design | 2-3 months | Engineering plans, specifications | Design approval |
| 4. Permitting | 2-6 months | Licenses, permits, approvals | Construction authorization |
| 5. Construction | 6-12 months | Built facility, installed equipment | Commissioning readiness |
| 6. Commissioning | 1-2 months | Operational systems, validated performance | Operations handover |
| 7. Ramp-Up | 6-12 months | Full production capacity | Commercial operation |

**Total Timeline:** 18-36 months from concept to full production

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Feasibility (2-4 weeks)

### Objectives
- Assess project concept viability
- Identify potential fatal flaws
- Develop preliminary scope and budget
- Determine if detailed feasibility study is warranted

### Activities

| Activity | Duration | Responsibility | Output |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| Initial stakeholder meeting | 1-2 days | Project team | Meeting notes, objectives |
| Market overview | 3-5 days | Analyst | Market summary report |
| Site reconnaissance | 2-3 days | Technical team | Site photos, initial assessment |
| Preliminary financial screening | 3-5 days | Financial analyst | Order-of-magnitude estimates |
| Risk identification | 1-2 days | Project team | Initial risk register |
| Pre-feasibility report | 3-5 days | Project manager | Pre-feasibility summary |

### Deliverables

**Pre-Feasibility Report Contents:**
1. Executive summary and recommendation
2. Project concept description
3. Market opportunity summary
4. Preliminary site assessment
5. Technology options overview
6. Order-of-magnitude CAPEX and OPEX
7. High-level financial indicators
8. Critical risk factors
9. Go/no-go recommendation
10. Scope for feasibility study

### Decision Gate

**Proceed to Feasibility if:**
- [ ] Market opportunity appears viable
- [ ] Suitable site available or identifiable
- [ ] Technology solution exists
- [ ] Financial returns potentially attractive
- [ ] No fatal flaws identified
- [ ] Stakeholder alignment confirmed

**Budget:** SAR 50,000-150,000

## Phase 2: Feasibility Study (1-2 months)

### Objectives
- Conduct thorough technical and financial analysis
- Confirm project viability
- Develop detailed business case
- Support investment decision

### Activities

| Activity | Duration | Responsibility | Output |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| Market analysis | 2-3 weeks | Analyst | Market study report |
| Site investigation | 1-2 weeks | Technical team | Site assessment report |
| Technology evaluation | 2-3 weeks | Technical team | Technology recommendation |
| Financial modeling | 2-3 weeks | Financial analyst | Financial model |
| Risk assessment | 1-2 weeks | Project team | Risk register and analysis |
| Regulatory review | 1-2 weeks | Regulatory specialist | Permitting roadmap |
| Feasibility report | 2-3 weeks | Project manager | Comprehensive feasibility study |

### Deliverables

**1. Market Study**
- Market size and growth projections
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Pricing analysis by channel
- Customer/buyer identification
- Market access strategy

**2. Technical Assessment**
- Site evaluation and suitability analysis
- Species selection and production plan
- System design concepts
- Equipment requirements
- Utility requirements
- Layout concepts

**3. Financial Model**
- Detailed CAPEX estimates
- OPEX projections
- Revenue forecasts
- Cash flow projections (monthly Year 1, annual Years 2-10)
- Financial metrics (NPV, IRR, payback)
- Sensitivity analysis
- Scenario analysis

**4. Risk Assessment**
- Technical risks
- Market risks
- Financial risks
- Operational risks
- Regulatory risks
- Risk mitigation strategies

**5. Implementation Plan**
- Project timeline
- Resource requirements
- Key milestones
- Critical path analysis

**6. Feasibility Report**
- Executive summary with recommendations
- Full analysis documentation
- Go/no-go recommendation
- Detailed next steps if proceeding

### Decision Gate

**Investment Decision Criteria:**
- [ ] IRR meets minimum threshold (typically >15%)
- [ ] NPV positive at target discount rate
- [ ] Payback period acceptable (typically <7 years)
- [ ] Technical solution proven and appropriate
- [ ] Market access secured or achievable
- [ ] Regulatory pathway clear
- [ ] Risks acceptable and manageable
- [ ] Funding structure achievable

**Budget:** SAR 200,000-500,000

## Phase 3: Detailed Design (2-3 months)

### Objectives
- Develop complete engineering designs
- Prepare specifications for construction and equipment
- Obtain cost certainty for CAPEX
- Prepare tender documents

### Activities

| Activity | Duration | Responsibility | Output |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| Design kick-off | 1 week | Project team | Design brief, criteria |
| Process design | 3-4 weeks | Process engineer | P&IDs, mass/energy balances |
| Civil/structural design | 4-6 weeks | Civil engineer | Structural drawings, specs |
| Mechanical design | 4-6 weeks | Mechanical engineer | Equipment specs, layouts |
| Electrical design | 3-4 weeks | Electrical engineer | Electrical drawings, specs |
| Instrumentation design | 2-3 weeks | I&C engineer | SCADA specs, control logic |
| Design integration | 2 weeks | Design team | Integrated design package |
| Cost estimate | 2 weeks | Quantity surveyor | Class 2 cost estimate |
| Tender preparation | 2 weeks | Project manager | Tender documents |

### Deliverables

**Engineering Deliverables:**

| Category | Documents |
|----------|-----------|
| Process | P&IDs, process flow diagrams, mass balances, equipment datasheets |
| Civil | Site plans, foundation drawings, tank drawings, building drawings |
| Mechanical | Equipment layouts, piping plans, HVAC drawings |
| Electrical | Single line diagrams, cable schedules, lighting plans |
| Instrumentation | Instrument lists, loop diagrams, SCADA specifications |

**Specifications:**
- RAS equipment specifications
- Building specifications
- Utility specifications
- Control system specifications

**Tender Package:**
- Instructions to bidders
- General conditions
- Technical specifications
- Bill of quantities
- Drawing list
- Contract form

**Cost Estimate:**
- Class 2 estimate (±15% accuracy)
- Detailed quantity takeoffs
- Vendor quotations
- Contingency analysis

### Design Review Checkpoints

**30% Design Review:**
- Process basis confirmed
- Major equipment selected
- Building layout finalized
- Continue to detailed design?

**60% Design Review:**
- P&IDs complete
- Major specifications complete
- Electrical loads confirmed
- Address design issues

**90% Design Review:**
- All drawings complete
- All specifications complete
- Cost estimate updated
- Ready for tender?

### Decision Gate

**Design Approval Criteria:**
- [ ] All engineering disciplines complete
- [ ] Design meets production requirements
- [ ] Cost estimate within budget
- [ ] Constructability reviewed
- [ ] Safety review completed
- [ ] Owner review and approval

**Budget:** SAR 500,000-1,500,000 (3-5% of project CAPEX)

**See also:** [Design Phase Checklist](./design_phase_checklist.md)

## Phase 4: Permitting and Approvals (2-6 months)

### Objectives
- Obtain all required permits and licenses
- Secure regulatory approvals
- Clear construction authorization

### Activities

| Activity | Duration | Responsibility | Output |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| Permit identification | 2 weeks | Regulatory specialist | Permit checklist |
| Application preparation | 4-8 weeks | Project team | Complete applications |
| Environmental review | 4-8 weeks | Environmental consultant | EIA (if required) |
| Agency submissions | Variable | Regulatory specialist | Submitted applications |
| Agency coordination | Ongoing | Project team | Approvals |
| Building permits | 4-8 weeks | Contractor | Building permit |

### Regulatory Requirements (Saudi Arabia)

**Ministry of Environment, Water and Agriculture (MEWA):**
- Aquaculture license
- Water use permit
- Environmental approval

**Saudi Food and Drug Authority (SFDA):**
- Food facility registration
- Product approval

**Municipal/Local:**
- Building permit
- Commercial license
- Utility connections

**Other (as applicable):**
- Agricultural Development Fund (ADF) loan approval
- Foreign investment license (MISA)
- Fire department approval

### Naama Digital Platform

Saudi Arabia's Naama system streamlines aquaculture permitting:
- Online application submission
- Document upload
- Status tracking
- Integrated approvals

**Typical Timeline:**
- Application preparation: 2-4 weeks
- MEWA processing: 4-8 weeks
- Total: 2-4 months (improved from previous 6-12 months)

### Decision Gate

**Construction Authorization:**
- [ ] Aquaculture license obtained
- [ ] Environmental approval received
- [ ] Building permit issued
- [ ] Utility connection agreements
- [ ] Financing confirmed
- [ ] Contractor selected

**Budget:** SAR 100,000-300,000 (permits, consultants, fees)

### Objectives
- Build facility according to design
- Install all equipment

### Activities

**Phase 5A: Site Work (2-4 weeks)**

- Access roads
- Fencing and security
- Temporary facilities

- Concrete foundations

- Tank bases

- Roof and wall installation
- Interior partitions

**Phase 5D: Mechanical Systems (8-12 weeks)**

- HVAC installation

- Electrical distribution
- Lighting
- Control panels

- Tank hydrostatic testing

- System integration testing

**Documentation:**
- Daily construction logs
- Inspection reports
- Test certificates
- As-built drawings

**Construction Completion:**
- [ ] All structures complete

## Phase 6: Commissioning (1-2 months)

### Objectives
- Validate all systems operate correctly
- Establish biofilter
- Train operations staff
- Achieve handover to operations

### Activities

| Activity | Duration | Responsibility | Output |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| Pre-commissioning checks | 1-2 weeks | Commissioning team | Checklists complete |
| Utility commissioning | 1 week | Contractors | Utilities operational |
| Equipment commissioning | 2-3 weeks | Vendors/team | Equipment operational |
| System integration | 1-2 weeks | Commissioning team | Integrated operations |
| Biofilter establishment | 4-8 weeks | Operations team | Active biofilter |
| Staff training | 2-4 weeks | Trainer/manager | Trained team |
| Trial stocking | 2-4 weeks | Operations team | Initial fish in system |
| Performance testing | 1-2 weeks | Project team | Performance verified |
| Handover | 1 week | Project team | Operations responsibility |

### Commissioning Sequence

**1. Pre-Commissioning**
- Verify construction completion
- Clean all systems
- Calibrate all instruments
- Test all controls

**2. Utility Commissioning**
- Electrical system energization
- Water supply testing
- Backup generator testing
- HVAC commissioning

**3. Equipment Commissioning**
- Individual equipment startup
- Pump testing
- Blower testing
- UV system verification
- Heater/chiller testing

**4. System Commissioning**
- Fill tanks with water
- Circulate through treatment loop
- Verify flows and pressures
- Test all alarms
- Verify SCADA function

**5. Biofilter Establishment**
- Seed with nitrifying bacteria
- Add ammonia source
- Monitor nitrogen cycle
- Build bacterial population (4-8 weeks)
- Confirm ammonia/nitrite removal capacity

**6. Trial Stocking**
- Stock small number of fish (10-20% capacity)
- Monitor water quality closely
- Verify system stability
- Adjust operations as needed

### Training Program

**Operations Training:**
- RAS fundamentals
- SOPs for all procedures
- Equipment operation
- Emergency procedures
- Documentation requirements

**Training Duration:** 2-4 weeks intensive

### Decision Gate

**Handover Criteria:**
- [ ] All systems operational
- [ ] Biofilter established and stable
- [ ] Trial stocking successful
- [ ] Staff trained and competent
- [ ] SOPs finalized
- [ ] Documentation complete
- [ ] Certifications obtained (SFDA, BAP)

**Budget:** SAR 200,000-500,000

**See also:** [Operations Handover Checklist](./operations_handover_checklist.md)

## Phase 7: Ramp-Up Operations (6-12 months)

### Objectives
- Increase production to full capacity
- Optimize operations and efficiency
- Achieve commercial profitability
- Establish market presence

### Activities

| Activity | Duration | Responsibility | Output |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| Initial production cycle | 4-6 months | Operations team | First harvest |
| Process optimization | Ongoing | Operations team | Improved KPIs |
| Staff development | Ongoing | Manager | Competent team |
| Market development | Ongoing | Sales/manager | Customer base |
| Capacity increase | 6-12 months | Operations team | Full capacity |

### Production Ramp-Up Schedule

**Typical Ramp-Up (200-ton/year facility):**

| Period | Capacity Utilization | Production (tons) | Key Focus |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Month 1-3 | 20-30% | 10-15 | Learning, system tuning |
| Month 4-6 | 40-50% | 20-25 | First harvest, market entry |
| Month 7-9 | 60-70% | 30-35 | Optimization, efficiency |
| Month 10-12 | 80-90% | 40-45 | Full stocking, steady state |
| Year 2 | 90-100% | 180-200 | Commercial operation |

### Key Milestones

**Month 3:**
- First cohort at market size (if fast-growing species)
- All SOPs tested and refined
- Water quality stable
- Feed management optimized

**Month 6:**
- First commercial harvest
- Sales channels established
- FCR within target range
- Mortality within target range

**Month 12:**
- Near-full capacity operation
- Profitable operations (EBITDA positive)
- Established customer relationships
- Experienced operations team

### Performance Targets (Year 1)

| Metric | Month 1-3 | Month 4-6 | Month 7-12 | Year 2+ |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------------|---------|
| FCR | <2.0 | <1.8 | <1.6 | <1.5 |
| Mortality | <10% | <8% | <5% | <3% |
| Capacity | 30% | 50% | 80% | 100% |
| WQ Compliance | 90% | 95% | 99% | 99%+ |

### Common Ramp-Up Challenges

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| Biofilter instability | Conservative stocking, close monitoring |
| High mortality | Enhanced biosecurity, water quality focus |
| Poor FCR | Feed management training, optimization |
| Equipment issues | Aggressive troubleshooting, vendor support |
| Market access | Pre-arranged offtake, diversified channels |
| Staff turnover | Competitive compensation, training investment |

## Timeline Planning

### Critical Path Analysis

**Critical Path Elements:**
1. Feasibility study completion → Investment decision
2. Design completion → Tender issuance
3. Permits obtained → Construction start
4. Equipment procurement → Installation readiness
5. Construction completion → Commissioning start
6. Biofilter establishment → First stocking
7. Growth period → First harvest

### Dependencies

```
Feasibility ──→ Investment Decision ──→ Detailed Design
                                              │
                                              ▼
                              Permitting ←── Design Complete
                                 │
                                 ▼
Equipment Procurement ───→ Construction ───→ Commissioning
                                              │
                                              ▼
                            Biofilter Est. ──→ Stocking ──→ Ramp-Up
```

### Parallel Activities

**Can Run in Parallel:**
- Permitting and detailed design (latter part)
- Equipment procurement and construction
- Staff recruitment and construction
- Marketing development and construction

### Contingency Planning

**Schedule Buffers:**
- Design: 2-4 weeks contingency
- Permits: 4-8 weeks contingency
- Construction: 8-12 weeks contingency
- Commissioning: 2-4 weeks contingency

**Total Contingency:** 15-25% of base timeline

## Risk Management by Phase

### Phase-Specific Risks

| Phase | Key Risks | Mitigation |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| Pre-Feasibility | Overlooked fatal flaw | Thorough checklist, expert review |
| Feasibility | Inaccurate estimates | Conservative assumptions, contingency |
| Design | Scope creep, design errors | Change control, design reviews |
| Permitting | Delays, rejections | Early engagement, complete submissions |
| Construction | Cost overruns, delays | Fixed-price contracts, schedule monitoring |
| Commissioning | System failures | Testing, vendor support, contingency |
| Ramp-Up | Low production, high mortality | Conservative stocking, expert support |
